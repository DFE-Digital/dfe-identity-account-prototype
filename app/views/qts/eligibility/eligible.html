You’re eligible to apply for qualified teacher status (QTS) in England

Based on the information you’ve given us you can apply for QTS.

Preparing to apply

If you decide to apply, you'll need to provide a range of evidence. We'll ask you to upload some scans, photos, or original files.

You may want to spend some time gathering all the documents you need before you begin your application.

What we’ll ask for

You’ll need to provide the following evidence when you apply.

1. Proof of identity

You must provide:

  • A passport, national identity card or driving licence that shows your date of birth and nationality. This must be valid and have not expired.

If the name on your proof of identity is different from that on your other documents, you need to provide evidence of your name change (for example, a divorce certificate or name change deed).

2. Proof of qualifications

To apply for QTS, you must hold a teaching qualification that’s equivalent to a ‘level 6’ qualification in the UK. The assessor will need to review the level and the duration of your qualification.

Learn more about qualification levels in the UK.

They’ll also need to understand the extent to which your qualification was focussed on developing practical teaching skills. As evidence, you must provide:

You must provide:

  • Your certificate from the qualification that led to you being a recognised teacher (this might be your university degree, or a separate qualification).
  • A transcript (or diploma supplement) for that qualification, issued by the awarding body. If your teaching qualification was part of your university degree, use your degree transcript.

A transcript is a document from the institution where you studied. It lists all the modules or subjects you studied in each year, and the marks or grades you were awarded. Note that you must have completed all of your teaching qualification in {{ data["country"] }}.

3. Proof of English language ability

We need to understand your level of English language proficiency. You can provide evidence in any of the following ways:

  • Show you were born or hold citizenship of a country that’s exempt from English language requirements

    You can show this when you upload a copy of your passport or the official identification document for that country.

  • Show you were taught in English in a country that’s exempt from English language requirements

    You can show this when you upload the transcript from your degree qualification.

  • Show you were taught in English, but in a country that’s NOT exempt from English language requirements

    Upload the ‘Medium of instruction’ (MOI) from your degree qualification. You can get this by contacting the institution where you studied.

  • Show you hold an approved English language certificate

    You’ll need a Secure English Language Test (SELT) at level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) scale. This is higher than the B1 proficiency the UK Home Office requires when people apply for UK citizenship, because you’ll be instructing students in English.

    The test must be on the list of approved English language tests and awarded within the last 2 years before the date of your application.

Learn more about level B2 English language.

Find out about approved test providers.

What about multiple nationalities?

If you’re a national of more than 1 country and both are exempt from English language requirements, you can choose which of them you use to apply for QTS. Remember, you must be able to show a passport (or the recognised identity document for that country).

If you hold a second nationality that is not exempt (and you apply for QTS using that nationality) you’ll need to prove your English language proficiency using one of the other methods.

4. Proof of your work history

You’ll need to show you’ve been employed as a teacher in a school for 1 UK school year (9 months of active teaching time) or more.

We’ll ask you to provide the name and email address of a contact who we can ask to confirm that your professional work experience:

  • was in a full-time role where your main function was as a teacher (rather than other responsibilities in school)
  • was gained working with children aged 5-16 (although we can accept experience with children aged 3-4 or 17-19 if the educational setting also included children aged 5-16)
  • involved being responsible for a minimum of 4 children

If the assessor recommends awarding QTS, you understand that we’ll contact the references you’ve provided to verify your work history before the award is issued.

Note that as a teacher with more than 1 year of professional experience, but less than 2 years, you’ll need to complete a 2-year ‘statutory induction’ period as a newly qualified teacher.

Learn more about statutory induction periods for newly qualified teachers.

5. Proof that you’re recognised as a teacher

As your education department or authority has an online register of teachers, you’ll need to provide any reference numbers we need to check your record.

The education department or authority must also confirm in writing:

  • that your authorisation to teach has never been suspended, barred, cancelled, revoked or restricted, and that you have no sanctions against you

You’ll need to provide a Letter of Professional Standing, which you can obtain by contacting:

Teachers Registration Board of South Australia (TRBSA)

Level 6, 70 Pirie Street, Adelaide, South Australia 5000, Australia

info@trb.sa.edu.au

https://www.trb.sa.edu.au

This must be dated within 3 months of you applying for QTS.

6. Certified translations

If your documents are not written in English, you must provide certified translations (at your own cost) for your:

  • qualifications
  • transcripts
  • proof that you’re recognised as a teacher

You do not need translations for ID or change of name documents.

About certified translations

Translations must be carried out by a certified translator, who must state (on the document or a separate certification page) that the translation is a true, accurate and correct rendering of the original.

Each one must include the date of the translation as well as the certified translator’s:

  • signature
  • name and contact details
  • official certification seal/stamp or ID number

Finding a certified translator

We cannot recommend translation providers. You may want to explore translators who are members of the Institute of Translating and Interpretation (opens in a new tab). Sometimes a university’s language department can also help.
