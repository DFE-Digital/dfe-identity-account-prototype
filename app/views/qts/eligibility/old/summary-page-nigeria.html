You’re eligible to apply for qualified teacher status (QTS) in England

Based on the information you’ve given us you can apply for QTS.

Preparing to apply

If you decide to apply, you’ll need to provide a range of evidence. We’ll ask you to upload some scans, photos, or original files.

You may want to spend some time gathering all the documents you need before you begin your application. Once you start your application, we’ll sign you out if you’re inactive for 60 minutes.

What we’ll ask for

You’ll need to provide the following evidence when you apply.

1. Proof of identity

You must provide either a:

  • passport (must not have expired)
  • birth certificate
  • national identity card that shows your date of birth and nationality (must not have expired)

2. Proof of qualifications

You must hold a teaching qualification that’s equivalent to a UK bachelor degree (‘level 6’) or higher.

Check you hold a qualification that is equivalent or higher than a level 6.

We’ll need to understand how much of your qualification was focussed on developing practical teaching skills.

You’ll need to provide evidence of your teaching qualification:

  • certificate (this might be your university degree, or other qualification)
  • transcript (or diploma supplement)

3. Proof of English language ability

If you were born or hold citizenship of any of the countries below, you’re exempt from English language requirements. You’ll need to provide your passport or official identification documents for that country as proof.

  • Antigua and Barbuda
  • Australia
  • The Bahamas
  • Barbados
  • Belize
  • Canada
  • Dominica
  • Grenada
  • Guyana
  • Jamaica
  • Ireland
  • Malta
  • New Zealand
  • St Kitts and Nevis
  • St Lucia
  • St Vincent and the Grenadines
  • Trinidad and Tobago
  • USA
  • England
  • Scotland
  • Northern Ireland
  • Wales

If you were not born or do not hold citizenship from any of the exempt countries listed above, you can provide evidence of your English language ability in any of the following ways:

If your teaching qualification or university degree was taught in an exempt country (listed above)

Upload the transcript from your teaching qualification or university degree (which you can request from the institution where you studied).

If your teaching qualification or university degree was taught completely in English, but in a country that’s NOT exempt (not listed above)

Upload a document that shows your teaching qualification or university degree was taught exclusively in the English language – this is an official document that you can request from the institution where you studied.

If you have an approved English language certificate

You’ll need a Secure English Language Test (SELT) at level B2 of the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) scale. The test must be on the list of approved English language tests and awarded within the last 2 years before the date of your application.

Find an approved test provider if you need to get an English language certificate.

4. Proof of your work history

You need to show you’ve been employed as a teacher in a school for 1 UK school year (9 months of active teaching time) or more.

You’ll need to provide the name and email address of a contact who can confirm that during your professional work experience you:

  • worked unsupervised with children aged between 5 and 16 years.
  • were solely responsible for planning, preparing and delivering lessons to at least 4 students at a time.
  • were solely responsible for assessing and reporting on the progress of those students.

We’ll contact the references you’ve provided to verify your work history before we award QTS.

6. Certified translations

If your documents are not written in English, you must provide certified translations (at your own cost) for your:

  • qualifications
  • transcripts
  • proof that you’re recognised as a teacher

You do not need translations for ID or change of name documents.

About certified translations

Translations must be completed by a certified translator, who must state (on the document or a separate certification page) that the translation is a true, accurate and correct copy of the original.

Each one must include the date of the translation as well as the certified translator’s:

  • signature
  • name and contact details
  • official certification seal/stamp or ID number

Finding a certified translator

We cannot recommend translation providers. You may want to explore translators who are members of the Institute of Translating and Interpretation (opens in a new tab). Sometimes a university’s language department can also help.
